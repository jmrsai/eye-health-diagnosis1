## üìö Educational Case Studies

### Case Study 1: Diabetic Retinopathy Detection
**Patient Profile**: 58-year-old male with Type 2 diabetes for 12 years

**Presentation**:
- Blurred vision in left eye
- Difficulty reading small text
- No pain or flashing lights

**AI Analysis Results**:
- Confidence: 94.7%
- Findings: Moderate non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy
- Risk Factors: HbA1c 8.2%, hypertension, 12-year diabetes duration
- Recommendations: Immediate ophthalmologic referral, improved glycemic control

**Clinical Outcome**: 
- Confirmed moderate NPDR on dilated fundus exam
- Initiated anti-VEGF therapy
- 6-month follow-up showed stabilization

### Case Study 2: Glaucoma Risk Assessment
**Patient Profile**: 45-year-old African American female

**Presentation**:
- Family history of glaucoma (mother)
- Routine eye exam
- No visual symptoms

**AI Analysis Results**:
- IOP: 24 mmHg (right), 22 mmHg (left)
- Cup-to-disc ratio: 0.6 bilateral
- Risk Score: 78% (high risk)
- Recommendations: Comprehensive glaucoma evaluation, visual field testing

**Clinical Outcome**:
- Visual field defects detected
- Diagnosed with early primary open-angle glaucoma
- Started on topical prostaglandin analog

### Case Study 3: Age-Related Macular Degeneration
**Patient Profile**: 72-year-old Caucasian male

**Presentation**:
- Central vision distortion
- Difficulty recognizing faces
- Straight lines appear wavy

**AI Analysis Results**:
- Drusen detected in both eyes
- Geographic atrophy in left macula
- Wet AMD suspected in right eye
- Urgency: High - requires immediate evaluation

**Clinical Outcome**:
- Confirmed wet AMD with choroidal neovascularization
- Initiated monthly anti-VEGF injections
- Visual acuity improved from 20/80 to 20/40
